Panic: XIII Minutes Explore Internal Conflict and Mental Warfare

XIII Minutes’ new single “Panic” drops this Friday, April 10. The more I listen to it, the more I love it which is wild, because I loved it the very first time I heard it.

As a Nu Metalhead, it checks off 90% of the elements that make me love a song. Personally, the only thing I might have added would be a couple of DJ scratches in a few spots, but that’s just me being a product of late ’90s and early 2000s Nu Metal with bands like Linkin Park and Limp Bizkit. This track definitely gives off some Linkin Park vibes.

The growls that emerge after the second chorus, continue through the third, and carry into the song’s closing moments really emphasize the seriousness of this internal battle and add another level of intensity to the song.

I love how the track starts out heavy, but as the first verse drops, it slightly mellows and brings in a sick beat, giving true Nu Metal vibes with clear hip-hop influence. As the verse progresses, the pace begins to pick up right before the song goes all in for the chorus. This structure perfectly represents the creeping effects of anxiety, building tension until it finally erupts — just as the lyrics suggest.

As a Christian, I relate with these lyrics, and I think many of us do. Even the Apostle Paul wrote about how he desired to do what was right but found himself struggling against his own nature, saying, “For I do not do the good I want to do, but the evil I do not want to do — this I keep on doing,” capturing the same internal conflict and battle within that these lyrics so powerfully express.

We all have a “dead man” inside us fighting to come back through.

Basically, I’m saying… Panic will preach.
